Research

What I work on

BigBrain

Splicing QTL mapping in 10,000+ human brain samples

Multi-ancestry random-effects meta-analysis across 43 tissue-cohort pairs to map genetic effects on RNA splicing.

52,696 cis-sQTLs identified
Nanopore · ONT

Direct long-read RNA sequencing of LCLs

Native poly(A) RNA-seq of 60 genetically diverse LCLs revealing novel transcript and m⁶A modification QTLs.

trQTLs · m⁶A-QTLs
Disease genetics

Splicing as a mediator of neurological disease risk

Colocalization of sQTLs with GWAS for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Schizophrenia and other brain disorders.

102 GWAS loci · TREM2 · BIN3

About me

Beyond the data

Origins

I grew up in the Aosta Valley in the Italian Alps — a tiny mountain region tucked between France, Switzerland and Italy. Growing up surrounded by mountains gave me a deep love for the outdoors, a strong sense of independence, and a slightly obsessive relationship with good food. I did my PhD at the University of Geneva, then moved to New York for my postdoc — going from alpine villages to Manhattan is quite a jump, but I love it.

Life in New York

New York has been a wonderful surprise. I love the energy, the food scene, and the ability to do everything on foot. Outside the lab you will find me running, doing Pilates, cycling, cooking elaborate Italian meals, or exploring a new neighborhood — currently convinced that the East Village is having a moment and I am here for it.

Running Pilates Cycling Cooking Exploring NYC

Mountains & skiing

Growing up in the Alps means skiing is less a hobby and more a life skill. I have been on skis since before I could properly read. Going back to the mountains every winter is non-negotiable — it is where I recharge and remember that not everything has to be optimized and p-valued.

Handywoman

I am the designated repair person in my family — they wait for me to come home even to change a light bulb. I genuinely enjoy fixing things, assembling furniture, and figuring out why something stopped working. It is not unlike debugging code, except the error messages are more cryptic and the tools are heavier.

Travelling & national parks

I love to travel and since moving to the US I have been slowly discovering American national parks — a project that is going to take a very long time and I am completely fine with that. So far: Grand Canyon, Zion, Bryce Canyon, Acadia. Next on the list: Glacier and the Pacific Northwest. Europe will always have my heart but the American landscape is on another scale entirely.
