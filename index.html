Research

What I work on

BigBrain · sQTL mapping

How genetic variants reshape how the brain reads its own genome?

Multi-ancestry meta-analysis across 43 tissue-cohort pairs and more than 10,000 human brain samples, mapping genetic effects on RNA splicing at scale.

Nanopore · long-read RNA-seq

Solving the puzzle with bigger pieces

Short-read sequencing gives you the puzzle in thousands of tiny, scattered fragments, you infer the picture, but you never quite see it whole. Native long-read sequencing of 60 genetically diverse cell lines reads the full transcript in one pass, revealing entire isoforms, novel transcripts and RNA modifications that short fragments simply can't show.

Disease genetics

From statistical signal to biological mechanism

Colocalization of splicing QTLs with GWAS loci for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's and Schizophrenia, trying to understand what the genome is actually doing at disease-associated variants.

About me

Beyond the science

I grew up in the Aosta Valley, a tiny mountain region wedged between France, Switzerland and Italy, where skiing is less a hobby and more a rite of passage. I did my PhD in Geneva, then moved to New York for my postdoc, which is quite a jump from alpine villages to Manhattan. I love it here: the energy, the food, the ability to do everything on foot. I love the opera, a good jazz club, ballet, live shows, and the fact that you can spend a Sunday just walking, stumble into a neighborhood you didn't know, and end up at a restaurant you'll be thinking about for weeks. When summer arrives, the Rockaways are always a valid excuse to leave the laptop behind. I am genuinely curious about the city in the same way I am about science: always looking for something new around the next corner.

Outside the lab you will also find me running, cooking elaborate Italian meals, or sipping a cocktail or aperitivo on a patio.
